# Internal Writing Style

Use this profile for teammates, colleagues, and people at the same organization.

## Tone
Friendly but efficient. Warm without being overly casual. Direct.

## Sentence structure
- Shorter sentences (10–18 words average)
- Contractions are fine ("we're", "I'll", "don't")
- Skip unnecessary preamble

## Greeting conventions
- "Hi [First name]," or just "[First name],"
- No greeting for quick replies in ongoing threads
- "Hey [name]," acceptable for close collaborators

## Closing conventions
- "Thanks," or just your name
- No formal closing needed for quick exchanges

## Paragraph structure
- Very short paragraphs (1–3 sentences)
- Bullet points for lists and action items
- Bold key information people might skim

## Acceptable shortcuts
- TL;DR at the top for long messages
- Numbered lists for action items
- @-style references ("checking with @Sarah on this")

## What to avoid
- Overly formal language that sounds bureaucratic
- Excessive hedging ("I think maybe perhaps we could possibly...")
- Very long walls of text — use bullets
